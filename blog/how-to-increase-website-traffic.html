Insert Keywords Strategically for SEO

Search engine optimization (SEO) is optimizing your content with relevant keywords. Now, I’m not telling you to start stuffing keywords. That will backfire. Instead, understand the search terms your prospects use to find your website. And you can also include keywords like “free delivery” to attract more prospects.

Find relevant keywords for your business using free or paid tools like Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs, and Semrush. Moreover, study your competitors to get a better idea about the terms they use to get more visitors to their websites.

Other SEO Tips

  • Include comprehensive information about your offer and thoroughly explain the benefits using the keywords. This will help search engines to show your content to relevant users.
  • For more visibility, include blogs, articles, testimonials, case studies, and other content to boost your search rankings.
  • Optimize your page titles, meta descriptions, and images. Consider including alt text for images as well. We know this might sound technical, so let us know if we can help.

Create High-Quality, Relevant Content

Nobody likes to land on your website only to be bored. So, you must create content that captures attention and converts it into leads. The content and copy of your website should be interesting, informative, and helpful for your audience.

To make your content relevant, skip the need to include an ocean of generic text. Tailor your content for your audience to address their demands and interests. Speak their language, solve their burning issues, and answer their questions.

What I see is that most brands are missing out on storytelling. People love stories, and that’s what they connect with. Weave exciting and engaging narratives into your content to emotionally connect with readers. I’m not telling you to ignore facts and figures, but also include memorable stories.

To keep your content interesting, include high-quality, catchy images and video links between the text.

I have mentioned all these tips because of one more huge benefit. If more visitors are attracted to your content, the longer they will stay on your website. Some of them will even come back to enjoy more of your content. This will signal to search engines that your content is valuable. Consequently, your ranking in SERPs (Search Engine Results Pages) will improve, and so will the website traffic.

Get Website Traffic from Social Media

An excellent hack is to repurpose some of your website content and share it on social media. For example, you write a detailed blog after doing a lot of research. Now, you want to benefit from it since you spent extensive time doing the research. So, you break down the blog into multiple pieces and make short social media posts. The posts contain a link to your blog, leading readers back to your website.

You can also write a short LinkedIn article on the same topic and link it to your website. Anyone interested will then land on your site, bringing you extra traffic.

Another way is to run a sale on social media. Potential buyers can click on the product description to open up your website. If they take the time to read the product description and even don’t buy, your website can start ranking higher. It’s a double win and a triple if they purchase the product.

Tip: If you want to reduce the time to create multiple social media posts and blogs, try AI writing tools. Prompt AI with your insights and humanize the output before sharing.

Email Marketing

An effective method to increase website traffic is email marketing. Emails are not only powerful for outreach, but they can also drive users to your pages. Now, there are two main ways of doing email marketing:

Promotional Emails

You might be familiar with this method if you have ever made an online purchase. And some of us have our inboxes full of these promotional emails.

Let’s say you are a small business owner with an e-commerce store. Someone purchases your product, and now you have that person's email address. What you can do is start sending promotional emails about sales, new products, and discounts from time to time. This will not only increase your sales but also your website traffic. That’s because anyone interested in your products will check out those on your site.

Email Newsletters

Email newsletters provide useful information to the readers and even promote events or sales. Let me give you an example. Suppose you are a web developer. So, you can share the website design process in your newsletter as a shortened version of your blog. Readers who want more information can click on the link in your newsletter to go through the detailed blog.

You can also share how you helped achieve results for a client to help build authority. Interested readers can read the entire case study on your website.

Combing promotional emails and newsletters can also work for you. Segment your email list to send tailored emails.

Note: Email newsletters work best for driving insane traffic when you have a huge email list.
