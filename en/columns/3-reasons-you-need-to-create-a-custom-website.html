3 Reasons for the high number of scammers in website development

10-07-2023

There are two main ways to create a website. The first method is to create a template based on a pre-made function and design framework. The second is to develop everything from scratch without templates. In this column, we will cover the benefits of custom websites and downsides of template based websites.
Reading this column will give you an understanding of the limitations of most template-based websites. You'll see how custom websites can go beyond these limits. Let's take a look at three reasons why you want to have a custom website.

First, unique branding.

Brand means a unique impression of a company's products and services. Branding refers to the overall process of creating a brand. The goal of branding is to give consumers a unique perception of the company.
The main purpose of the website is to maximize branding. All elements of the website, including layout, logo, color, typography, and images, should represent the unique brand of the company. The template comes in a pre-made form of these design parts. Depending on the template, the range of adjustable elements may vary, but most of them are constrained. This makes it difficult to implement a design that fits your company's brand.
On the other hand, custom websites have 100% control over their design. All visual elements of the website can be adjusted freely as desired. Design can express the uniqueness of the brand. Therefore, it is much more advantageous to imprint the brand on visitors than on a uniform template website.
Different branding is required to represent the unique value contained in a brand's products and services. Websites with impressions like you've seen before have limitations in revealing the brand's unique identity.

Second, no restrictions on functional implementation.

The template website is subject to functional restrictions. Websites can only be built within the scope of pre-made features. There are often restrictions on integrating payment systems, linking external business tools, search and filter capabilities, and the ability to provide customized content to users.
Here's a website of a law firm built from a template. There is a blog inside the website and there are 100 articles in various categories such as inheritance law, immigration law, and divorce law. Visitor Paul is curious about the U.S. immigration law. He'd like to read only posts related to immigration laws out of 100 articles. However, the blog page does not show anything to filter. The only way to navigate the page is to manually scroll it. He feels uncomfortable, so he wants to look around other law firms' websites.
Later, he found a custom-made law firm website. This website also has a professional blog inside. On the page, the inheritance law, immigration law, and divorce law are separated by tabs. The sorting function exists, allowing you to view posts in order of latest and popularity. Search is also possible, allowing you to look up posts around specific keywords. He is satisfied with the more convenient user experience compared to the previous website.
In this example, you can see how custom websites can implement features aimed at the needs of visitors. Because there are no technical limitations to developing the function. Websites that meet the needs of visitors will have a positive impact on the brand's impression. Good awareness of the brand is the starting point for creating demand for the products and services.

Third, faster loading time.

When you visit the website, you may have clicked go back or closed the window because of the long loading time. After three seconds of loading, most visitors leave the site. The latency that occurs when using a website has a significant impact on user satisfaction. Therefore, the difference between 1 and 2 seconds of loading time is very important when creating a website.
Template websites typically have slower loading times. To be precise, there are factors that can slow down loading speed, and it is almost impossible to control them. These includes;
First, an oversized image file. You need a blog thumbnail image of 400 x 300 pixels here. However, the original image you want to use is 4000 x 3000 pixels in size. In this case, you should reduce the loading speed by reducing the image to match the thumbnail size. This is called image optimization, and sometimes the template website does not support it. This causes loading unnecessarily large size image files which takes more time.
Next is JavaScript and CSS files. Don't be flustered by unfamiliar terms. Think of it simply as a file related to the appearance and behavior of the website. Templates often load these files even from unnecessary pages.
In addition, template websites are relatively slow to load due to inefficient code, unoptimized third-party plug-ins, and excessive external script file calls.
On the other hand, custom websites can optimize image files to the right size. You can design related files to load only where they are needed. You can reduce loading time by creating efficient code. You can have most control over all the technical aspects related to loading speed.
The loading speed is a factor that is directly related to the user's satisfaction. A website that loads fast will increase visitor satisfaction. High-satisfaction users have no choice but to have a good impression on the brand.
So far, we've covered three reasons why you want to have custom websites. The reasons are as follows.
  1. Unique branding
  2. No restrictions on functional implementation
  3. Faster loading speed
On the vast land of online, there are countless stores called websites. A store with a unique exterior can catch the eye of customers. A store that provides customers' needs and facilitates their convenience makes visitors happy. They want to visit the store again, where the road to visit is open without being blocked.
Creating websites that satisfy users is the starting point for online branding. The template website has the advantage of being relatively easy to create, low price and short production period. However, due to the inherent limitations of the template, there are restrictions on creating websites that fit the brand perfectly.
On the other hand, custom websites are customized for everything in design, function, and performance. It's relatively expensive and takes a long time to build, but it's optimized for representing the unique identity of the brand.
You can make a wise decision by knowing the difference between template based websites and custom websites.
