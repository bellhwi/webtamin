How to prevent fraud from website development

08-05-2023

This column is a follow-up to the previous column, '3 reasons for the high number of scammers in website development'. Please refer to this column for a detailed explanation of typical web development scams. Then let's look into how to prevent fraud from creating websites.

First, how to prevent ‘Eat-and-run’ fraud.

‘Eat-and-run’ is a type of fraud in which you invest a small amount of money to set up a company and then take the customer's advance money and disappear. The easiest way to determine the reliability of your business is to do so. If you have a web development business in mind, try the following.
  1. Access the website.
  2. Scroll down to the bottom of the website.
  3. Check whether there is any identification information such as the employee identification number(EIN) given by the government.
  4. If not, check other relevant pages, such as contacting, introducing, and so on.
Did you find it? Congratulations. The probability that the company is a ‘Eat-and-run’ type has decreased significantly.
But this alone is not conclusive. If you're a clever conman, you can even manipulate this. We need to look closely at other aspects as well.
The next step is to contact your old customer. Most web development websites have testimonials or reviews from previous customers. Select one of the cases and enter the website.
Find the contact information and send them an inquiry about the service of the company you are investigating. The inquiry may consist of a short survey about the name of the company and satisfaction level that you received. If you get a positive response, the reliability of the company will increase significantly.

Second, how to prevent ‘He cries wine and sells vinegar’ fraud.

The ‘He cries wine and sells vinegar’ is a type in which an incompetent individual steals other people's work and deceives customers as if they were experts. How can you tell the authenticity of a portfolio in a web development company?
As mentioned earlier, you can contact your old customer directly. But there's no guarantee of a reply. Even if they do, you don't know how long it will take.
There is a more direct way to verify. It requires the web development company to prove the previous work.
Typically, web development businesses keep previous work files of customers. Because it is necessary for post-maintenance. These files consist primarily of source code.
Source code is simply a file that creates the internal structure of a website. This allows you to load the same website that you asked for on your personal computer. And you can easily change what you see, such as the title, the body, and the color of the text.
Even if you don't understand this part well, there's no problem at all. It's enough to know that there's something like this. You can request the following from the company.
"We would like to inquire about the authenticity of your portfolio. Could you please record a short video of some source code modifications reflecting the changes on the website? I'd appreciate it if you could share it using the Google Drive link."
If there is no reply or if they refuse the request for an unconvincing reason, it is a red light.

Third, the prevention of rip-off fraud.

Rip-off is a type that charges excessively for basic services or requires unreasonable additional costs.
The first thing to do as a precaution is to do some legworks. We recommend that you request a quote from at least three companies. If you ask about 5 companies, you can know the average market price to some extent.
Quotations typically come with the proposals. It consists primarily of an overview of the service. Some places offer contracts more formally. It must contain a detailed description of the service in any format.
Is there a place that induces payment without a word of details? If so, quit that website without hesitation for a second.
Check whether there is a proposal or contract. What's important here is that you really have to 'check'. Don't skim through as if it's like the terms and conditions of membership or something on any website.
You should carefully read the project description, development period, development cost, refund conditions, maintenance, etc. You have to take your time to examine whether all the provisions are reasonable, whether there are any ambiguities, and whether there are any questions. Make sure there are additional costs for design, functionality, page count, hosting costs, etc.
If there are any expenses that you don't understand, you should ask. If any sentence in the contract feels clueless, you must ask for a clear explanation. If there is something you are not sure about, even if it's trivial, you should ask. If you find it annoying to go over the contract, it can cause a worse annoying situation in the future.
So far, we've looked at three typical scams in the web development industry. Let me sum up. In order to prevent fraud by requesting website development, please do the following.
  • Confirming Business Registration
  • Contact your old customer
  • Ask businesses to authenticate their portfolios
  • Find out at least 3 different companies' estimates
  • Review proposals and contracts
Don't just easily believe everything you see. Make sure to examine the credibility of the information provided by the business.
